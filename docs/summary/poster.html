A note from your neighbors

We have a fruit fly problem.
We can fix it.

The bin area has been breeding fruit flies — and they don't stay there. Two small habits from each of us will eliminate the problem entirely.

Lab studies confirm fruit flies mechanically transfer E. coli, Salmonella, and Listeria onto food — one landing is enough. They also leave excrement on surfaces they land on, and we're seeing that damage on the garage walls.

See the research

fruit-fly-resources.github.io/safety/summary

The fix takes about 5 seconds.

1

Seal your trash bag before tossing it

Tie it closed every time — even for small amounts. Open bags are the main breeding source.

2

Rinse cans & bottles before recycling

Sticky residue in bottles and cans is a prime breeding site. A quick rinse breaks the cycle.

We're currently paying for weekly professional bin cleaning that wouldn't be necessary otherwise — money that could go toward keeping dues flat or improving the building. More than the cost: this is our home. We can do better than this.

Most of us are already doing this right — thank you.
